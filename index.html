ABOUT ME

Science and Research

Rodrigo A. Moreira
https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7605-8722


My research lies at the interface between applied mathematics, modeling, and computational physics and chemistry. I develop advanced physical-mathematical methods to understand the physical-chemical and functional properties of organic biopolymers, e.g protein complexes and cellulose polymers. My ultimate endeavor is to ensure that my research leads to significant advances and impact clinical therapies. In particular, this unique combination simulation and experimental data shed new light into the understanding of the evolutionary mechanism followed by the various SARS-CoV-2 mutants.

I have pioneered a framework that combines for the first time data analysis from all-atom molecular dynamics (MD), computational algebraic topology and computational geometry with also quantum chemistry concepts. It enables identifying higher-order emergent topological and geometrical invariant from large biological complexes, which can also be used to create multiscale models. In particular, the simple contact map analysis and hydrogen bonds network, commonly used within the structural biophysics community to create multiscale models and effective interactions, are based on pairwise interaction only. My methodology captures high-order interaction in an accurate mathematical background.
